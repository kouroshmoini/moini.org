        <h2 style="margin-bottom:10px;">Revitalizing Hochelaga-Maisonneuve</h2>
        <p style="max-width:900px;">
          This project proposes the strategic redevelopment of a 42-hectare industrial zone in 
          Hochelaga-Maisonneuve, Montreal. Historically shaped by port-adjacent manufacturing and 
          logistics activity, the site today is characterized by large brownfield parcels, fragmented 
          street networks, limited green space, and constrained transit access. The objective was to 
          reimagine the area as a high-density, transit-oriented mixed-use district that integrates 
          climate adaptation, ecological resilience, and improved mobility equity.
        </p>

          <h3>Transit & Slope Feasibility Analysis</h3>
          <p style="max-width:900px;">
            A core component of the proposal was evaluating tram feasibility across competing alignments. 
            Assomption Boulevard was found to reach slopes of approximately 14%, significantly exceeding 
            the 6% operational threshold for tram systems. In contrast, Viau Street demonstrated a more 
            consistent and manageable slope profile. A targeted tunnel segment was introduced to address 
            steeper sections while minimizing surface disruption. This approach improves east-west 
            connectivity while avoiding duplication of existing metro infrastructure.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div style="margin-top:35px;">
          <h3>Urban Restructuring & Land Use Strategy</h3>
          <p style="max-width:900px;">
            Large industrial allotments were reorganized into finer-grained blocks to reconnect the site 
            with the surrounding residential fabric. A new street hierarchy and shared streets enhance 
            pedestrian permeability and multimodal access. The land use mix prioritizes residential density 
            while incorporating commercial corridors, institutional uses, and consolidated green 
            infrastructure to support a complete, climate-resilient community.
          </p>
        </div>
